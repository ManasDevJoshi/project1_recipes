Momo

Description

Momo is a type of steamed filled dumpling with origins from the Himalayan region that is popular in Tibet, Bhutan, Nepal and India.

In Nepal, where it is considered a national dish, they are usually served with a sauce known as achar influenced by the spices and herbs used within many South Asian cuisines.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (for the dough)
  • Water (for the dough)
  • Ground meat (such as chicken, lamb, or pork)
  • Finely chopped vegetables (like cabbage, carrots, or onions)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Green chilies (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves
  • Soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Cooking oil (for frying or steaming)

Steps

  1. Prepare the filling:

    Finely chop vegetables (e.g., cabbage, carrots, and onions). Cook minced meat (e.g., chicken or pork) with spices and seasonings. Mix the vegetables with the cooked meat or prepare a vegetarian filling with just the vegetables.

  2. Make the dough:

    In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, water, and a pinch of salt. Knead the dough until smooth and elastic. Let the dough rest for 30 minutes.

  3. Shape the momos:

    Roll the dough into small, thin circles. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over the filling and pleat the edges to seal the momo.

  4. Steam the momos:

    Grease a steamer basket or line it with lettuce leaves. Arrange the momos in the steamer, leaving space between them. Steam the momos for about 10-15 minutes until cooked through.

  5. Serve and enjoy:

    Transfer the steamed momos to a serving plate. Serve hot with a dipping sauce of your choice (e.g., soy sauce or tomato chutney). Enjoy the delicious momos!
